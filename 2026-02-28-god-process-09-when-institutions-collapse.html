The Pattern of Institutional Collapse

Institutions fail through predictably recurring sequence that organizational science has documented:

Here’s the brutal truth about why reform never works:

Jerry Pournelle discovered “The Iron Law of Bureaucracy”—in any organization, people devoted to the bureaucracy itself will always defeat people devoted to the original mission. It’s not corruption; it’s evolutionary pressure. The institution’s survival instinct systematically eliminates anyone who threatens its growth and self-preservation.

I’ve watched this happen over and over. Mission-driven school board candidates get elected, then discover they can’t change anything because the bureaucracy has captured the entire system. Reform-minded pastors get hired, then find themselves managing institutional politics instead of shepherding souls. Conservative politicians win office, then realize the administrative state controls actual policy regardless of elections.

The system isn’t broken—it’s working exactly as designed to preserve itself, not serve its stated purpose.

Phase 1: Mission Drift. Original purpose becomes secondary to institutional preservation. Bureaucratic process replaces functional outcomes. Internal politics dominates external service.

Phase 2: Corruption Integration. Corrupt practices become systematically normalized. Whistleblowing is punished rather than rewarded. Performance metrics become manipulatable rather than objective.

Phase 3: Reality Disconnect. Institutional narratives diverge completely from observable reality. Internal conflict becomes institutionally managed. Truth-telling becomes existential threat to institutional survival.

Phase 4: Collapse. Public trust evaporates. Competent people exit or are expelled. Institution becomes actively destructive rather than merely ineffective.

Current institutional status: Phase 3-4 collapse across multiple domains simultaneously—government, media, education, healthcare all exhibiting reality disconnect and active destructiveness.
