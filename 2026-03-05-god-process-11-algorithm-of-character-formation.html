Theological Clarification: When this series references theosis (divine participation), it follows ancient Orthodox doctrine as expressed by Church Fathers like Athanasius: “The Son became man so that man might participate in divine nature” based on “his divine power has granted to us all things that pertain to life and godliness, through the knowledge of him who called us to his own glory and excellence, by which he has granted to us his precious and very great promises, so that through them you may become partakers of the divine nature” (2 Peter 1:3-4). This means sanctification into Christlikeness (Romans 8:29), co-working with God (1 Corinthians 3:9), and reigning with Christ (Revelation 20:6)—not absorption into divinity, independent godhood, or loss of human nature.
