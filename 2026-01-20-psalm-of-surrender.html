A Psalm of Surrender and Repentance

Response: Lord, hear our cry

Lord, O Lord— my God and my King—

My path winds toward destruction, yet You promise life through turning. The grave calls out my name, but Your voice calls me home.

Death seeks me in the darkness— life finds me in the dawn.

I. Confession of Rebellion

I have shattered Your commandments, trampled Your statutes underfoot. I have spurned Your covenant, defied Your holy word.

What was forbidden, I have chosen— what was commanded, I have scorned.

Death is my just portion, the wages I have earned. Justice demands my condemnation, mercy alone can spare.

Like Adam reaching for forbidden fruit, I chose wisdom that brought only shame. Like Eve doubting Your good word, I believed lies sweeter than truth.

Response: Have mercy on us

I turned from Your counsel, walked in my own understanding. I plotted my course in pride, yet You sovereignly rule my steps.

My ways led to destruction— Your ways lead to life.

Selah

II. Cry of Desolation

Where shall I go? / What shall I do? I wander lost / in wilderness of my making.

Friends have turned their faces away, family have shut fast their doors. Those who loved me have departed, leaving echoes in empty rooms.

I sought companions in rebellion— I find myself utterly alone.

Response: You are our refuge

Who will receive the outcast? How can the exile return? Where is sanctuary for the broken? Who offers refuge to the lost?

My purse grows thin as winter, my debts mount like flood waters. Soon I shall have nothing left but these clothes upon my back.

I have squandered every blessing— I have nothing left to give.

I long to be like that shrewd steward, making friends with worldly wealth— But my hands hold no treasure to offer, no grace to pour on others.

III. The Turning

Help me, O Lord— I cry from the depths! Let me repent / with more than words, with heart broken / and contrite.

From the depths I call to You— from the heights, answer me!

Response: He hears the humble

You declared: “It is not good for anyone to dwell alone”— Yet I spurned every companion You sent, rejected love’s patient gift.

In arrogance I spurned Your gifts, in folly chose desert over streams. Now I taste the bitter fruit of my own stubborn choosing.

I chose the barren over blessing— I chose the curse over covenant.

Selah

IV. Surrender and Trust

No path remains but surrender— my way has brought only ruin. I come broken, stripped bare, with nothing but this cry:

My wisdom led to folly— Your folly leads to wisdom.

Response: Your mercy endures forever

Judge me not for destruction, O Lord, though justice demands it. For thus declares the LORD: “I take no pleasure in the death of the wicked, But desire that all should turn from darkness into light.”

I am turning, Lord—turning now, not from wisdom / but from need. Not because I’ve found the way, but because I’ve lost all others.

In my strength I only stumbled— in my weakness, find Your power.

You alone remain my hope, Your mercy my only plea. If You will receive me back, I’ll follow where You lead.

Response: He restores the penitent

V. Covenant of Faith

Though it mean dwelling alone, I will trust Your purposes. Though it mean having nothing, I will rest in Your provision. Though it mean starting over with empty, trembling hands—

In emptiness, find Your fullness— in nothing, find Your everything.

For thus says the LORD: “I know the plans I have for you— Plans for peace and not calamity, to give you hope and future When you cannot see the dawn.”

Selah

VI. Prayer of Restoration (From Psalm 51)

Create in me a clean heart, O God, renew a right spirit within me. Cast me not from Your presence, take not Your Spirit away.

Response: Restore us, O Lord

Restore the joy of Your salvation, uphold me with willing spirit. Then will I teach transgressors Your ways, and sinners will return to You.
